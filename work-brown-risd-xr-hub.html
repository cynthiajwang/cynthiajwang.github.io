OVERVIEW

Brown + RISD XR Hub is a student-led organization at Brown University and the Rhode Island School of Design that aims to explore VR and AR technologies through collaborative projects, workshops, and field trips.

ROLE

As the sole designer, I designed and prototyped a website for our club for both mobile and desktop formats, taking inspiration from a collaborative mood board my team and I previously developed.

Moodboard

Collaborative moodboard our Visual team developed over the months of April-May

The Challenge

This was my first time designing and prototyping a full website alone. Since I was given a lot of copy to include, I initially struggled with finding a balance between visual appeal and readability.

When I first designed the website, I didn’t know about Figma’s autolayout function (which greatly helps in the development stage) and was much less focused on the user-friendliness of my designs. At that time, my goal was solely to create an eye-catching website to garner more interest in our club. Reviewing my designs a month later, I instantly pinpointed issues with the readability and decided to redesign the website.

Establishing a Design System

COLOR PALETTE

I selected a dark background for a dark mode website, creating a more techy vibe and emulating the immersiveness of XR. In contrast, the light secondary color has a hint of pink to juxtapose the cooler-toned palette. Since XR is inherently exclusive in terms of availability and affordability, it was important to add playfulness to our palette. Purple helped create a futuristic, sci-fi look and was also less corporate than the blue and gray colors we originally came up with.

TYPOGRAPHY

After playing around with multiple font pairings, I settled with two more futuristic, techy typefaces paired with a more plain but clean font. Most of the techy fonts I came across were difficult to read despite looking cool. Since I was designing a club website, I prioritized readability and was still able to achieve a futuristic look.

Identifying Problems with Initial Designs

1

Readability

Improve the visual layout of the website by increasing overall font size, choosing more precise font hierarchies and spacing between texts, and using more column layouts to prevent text from spanning the entire width of a page.

2

Usability

Increase ease of use by putting buttons closer together to minimize mouse movement and on the right side of the screen to follow the natural mouse position of most people.

3

User Experience

Adding more mouse interactions by using various buttons to group information and decrease clutter while also providing a more meaningful and interesting experience to users interacting with the site.

Reflection

  1. Always keep your audience in mind

    Create for people, put yourself in the user's shoes. Always have good readability

  2. Finding a balance

    Just because your top priority isn't creating stunning visuals doesn't mean the visuals still can't be a priority. Maybe you find unique ways to incorporate both good design for people and eye-catching elements

  3. Keep iterating, but know when to stop
