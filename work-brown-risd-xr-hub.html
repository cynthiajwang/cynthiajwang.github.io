OVERVIEW

Brown + RISD XR Hub is a student-led organization at Brown University and the Rhode Island School of Design that aims to explore VR and AR technologies through collaborative projects, workshops, and field trips.

Timeline

May - July 2023

Location

Remote

ROLE

As the sole designer partaking in this project, I designed and prototyped a website for both mobile and desktop formats, taking inspiration from a collaborative moodboard my team and I previously created.

Moodboard

Collaborative moodboard developed over the months of April-May.

With little to no direction, I defined a few goals for this project based on discussions during our weekly meetings about the club's desired image and values and the technical limitations we faced.

Initial Challenges

This was my first time designing and prototyping a full website alone. Since I had a lot of copy to work with, I initially struggled with finding a balance between visual appeal and readability.

When I first designed the website, about a month before embarking on the redesign project, I was much less experienced with Figma and web design in general and didn't know much about auto layout, for example. At that time, my goal was solely to create an eye-catching website to garner more interest in our club. After reviewing my designs a month later, especially after working on a few UX projects for my internship, I was able to instantly pinpoint issues with the website that needed to be fixed.

Establishing a Design System

COLOR PALETTE

I selected a dark background for a dark mode website, creating a more techy vibe and emulating the immersiveness of XR. In contrast, the light secondary color has a hint of pink to juxtapose the cooler-toned palette. Purple helped create a futuristic, sci-fi look while being less corporate than the blue and gray colors we originally planned to work with.

TYPOGRAPHY

After playing around with multiple font pairings, I settled with two more futuristic, techy typefaces paired with a more plain but clean font. Most of the techy fonts I came across looked cool but were difficult to read. Since I was designing a club website, I prioritized readability and was still able to achieve a futuristic look.

Both the colors and typography helped make the website more accessible and less intimidating while still maintaining a fitting XR look.

Resolving Problems with Initial Designs

1

Readability

Improve the visual layout of the website by increasing overall font size, choosing more precise font hierarchies and spacing between texts, and using more column layouts to prevent text from spanning the entire width of a page.

2

Usability

Increase ease of use by putting buttons closer together to minimize mouse movement and on the right side of the screen to follow the natural mouse position of most people.

3

User Experience

Adding more mouse interactions by using various buttons to group information and reducing clutter while also providing a more meaningful and interesting experience to users interacting with the site.

Final Designs

Reflection

  1. Design for a user.

    This was the first project where I really had to consider what designing for usability would look like. I strived to better present the website information in a readable and accessible way, ensuring users could get all the information they needed out of the website and find more details on topics that piqued their interest.

  2. Ease the design to development handoff.

    While working on this redesign, I was also interning at a startup and learning web development in my free time. I learned about Figma's auto layout property and became much more comfortable with HTML/CSS, understanding the tools I needed to make the handoff to our development team easier. I made sure to use precise measurements and reuse as many components as I could for responsive design.

    *I'll be coordinating with our Tech Team lead throughout the semester to implement these designs. Check back in a while to see our official club website in action!

  3. Create constraints for yourself.

    Since I didn't have much to work with and we hadn't really fleshed out the details of our visual identity, I discovered the importance of establishing both the brand and visual directions myself to keep the website cohesive and unified. I could then cross-check between my work and the guidelines and modify my designs as needed.
